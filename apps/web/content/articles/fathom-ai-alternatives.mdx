If bots make you nervous, privacy is on your mind, and you want more than simple note-taking and unlimited free recordings, then it's time to start looking beyond Fathom AI.

## Quick Fathom AI Review

I tested Fathom AI for weeks, and tbh it does what it says on the tin. It's a meeting bot that transcribes your Zoom, Meet, or Teams calls and generates summaries pretty fast (we're talking 30 seconds after the call ends) and the CRM sync is actually super useful for sales teams.

But soon you start noticing the cracks.

- **They're training AI on your meetings.** Not "might be" - they are, unless you manually dig into settings and opt out. Most people have no idea this is happening.
- **You can end up in their database without ever signing up.** If someone uses Fathom in a meeting with you, your voice, email, and conversation content gets collected. Then they might market to you.
- **The desktop app is basically a recording widget.** There's no place to jot down your thoughts during the meeting.
- **No real-time transcription** means you're flying blind during calls, and it doesn't work without internet. Dead WiFi? Dead tool
- **That bot is always visible.** For sensitive client calls or therapy sessions, having "Fathom Notetaker" sitting in your participant list changes the dynamic.
- **And their unlimited free plan is actually quite limited.** If you're only after free transcription and call recordings, sure it might work, but the AI summaries are capped at five/month.

If any of that bothers you, here are the top Fathom AI alternatives that you should definitely check out.

## 5 Best Fathom Alternatives: Detailed Reviews

### 1. Char: For When Privacy Actually Matters

Full disclosure - I'm the co-founder of [Char](/). But that's exactly why I built it. After seeing how every other tool handles your data, I knew there had to be a better way.

**Here's what makes it different:** Everything stays on your Mac. Not "encrypted in transit" or "stored securely" - I mean your conversations literally never leave your computer. Char uses local AI models (we built a custom 1.1GB model called HyperLLM-V1) that run entirely on-device.

You get a real notepad interface. Open Char and you see... a notepad. You can type notes during meetings, and the AI enhances them with context from the transcript. Real-time transcription shows up while people are talking, not after.

**Best For:** Healthcare providers, legal professionals, financial advisors, government agencies, therapists, and anyone handling confidential conversations where data sovereignty isn't negotiable.

- Real-time transcription during meetings (unlike Fathom's post-meeting only approach)

## Final Verdict: Which Is the Best Fathom AI Alternative?

### Pick based on what actually matters to you

**If privacy matters at all, use Char.** Full stop. Nothing else keeps your data truly private. Healthcare, legal, therapy, financial planning - anywhere conversations are confidential, this is the only real choice. Plus you get real-time transcription and offline support that Fathom can't match.

**If you're running a sales team and budget isn't tight, go with Avoma.** The automated CRM updates and call scoring actually improve team performance, not just transcribe calls. The coaching analytics are worth the price if you're serious about revenue. Just budget realistically - you'll need $70-110/user/month for features you actually want.

**If you need unified search across all communications, Read AI is interesting.** The ability to query meetings, emails, and Slack in one place is genuinely useful for product teams and executives tracking decisions across channels. But negotiate that Pro pricing - don't accept the first quote.

**If you want conversation analytics on a budget, Fireflies works.** The unlimited free transcription beats Fathom's 5-summary cap, and the pattern recognition across meetings is deeper. Just watch the storage limits and disable auto-sharing to all participants.

**If compliance certifications aren't optional, Sembly is your answer.** SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR out of the box. Regulated industries can't use tools without these, and Sembly was built for that from day one.
